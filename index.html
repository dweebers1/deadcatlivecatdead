You have a box.
You secure inside the box — a geiger counter.
You place inside of the geiger counter — a piece of radioactive material.

So little is the volume of the radioactive material that in the span of 1 hour —
the probability that one atom will decay = the probability that no atoms will decay.

If a single atom decays — the counter tube inside of the geiger instrument discharges —
triggering the release of a hammer that shatters a vial of hydrocyanic acid.

You have a cat.
You place the cat in the box.

In the course of 1 hour —
the probability that one atom will decay = the probability that no atoms will decay.

Therefore, in the course of 1 hour — 
the probability that the hydrocyanic acid has been released =
the probability that the hydrocyanic acid has not been released.

Therefore, in the course of 1 hour —
the probability that the cat is dead = the probability that the cat is alive.

For the duration of that hour, until the box is opened —
the cat is simultaneously alive and dead

You wait 1 hour.
You open the box.
